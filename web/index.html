XENFORGE terminology glossary
  • Global rank: Current protocol-wide participant counter used by mint mechanics.
  • cRank (claim rank): The rank captured in your mint receipt when you start a mint.
  • Term (days): How long your mint is locked before maturity.
  • Max selectable term: Highest term currently allowed by rank-based protocol rules.
  • AMP: Time-based multiplier that affects reward dynamics (shown as current protocol value).
  • APY (bps): Annual reward rate used for staking math, expressed in basis points (100 bps = 1.00%, so 2,000 bps = 20.00%). The percent is applied to staked principal over time. It does not directly set free-mint payout.
  • Maturity: Timestamp when a mint receipt becomes eligible to claim.
  • Pending claim: Receipt exists but maturity time has not been reached yet.
  • Claimable now: Receipt has matured and can be claimed.
  • Late penalty: Claim reduction that increases the longer you wait after maturity.
  • Mint receipt: On-chain object that stores your mint state (rank, term, maturity, AMP at claim).
  • On-chain vs simulation fallback: On-chain = direct RPC readback; fallback = local scenario data when RPC is unavailable.
  • Fee policy simulator: Planning tool only; not currently enforced on-chain.
Is there a pre-mint or team allocation here?

No. There is no pre-mint or team allocation in this build.

What determines mint outcomes?

Free-mint outcomes are driven by cRank vs current global rank (rank delta), selected term, AMP at claim, and claim timing/late-penalty band.

Is APY staking-related?

Yes. APY is primarily for staking reward calculations (rate on staked principal over term). Free-mint claim payout uses mint-specific terms (rank delta, term, AMP, and timing).

Why does timing matter?

Timing is a user control lever: claim near maturity to protect value. Waiting longer increases the late-claim reduction band.

What is the fee policy simulator for?

It is a planning tool for scenario checks (term, fee range, and outcome context). It is intentionally off-chain and does not execute transactions.

What fee formula does mint policy use?

Current policy model is linear by term-day: fee(day) = minFee + ((maxFee - minFee) × (365 - day)) / 364. Day 1 maps near max fee; day 365 maps to min fee.

Is XENFORGE audited?

No. Current build is unaudited and experimental.
