FAQ and glossary

What is XENFORGE?

XENFORGE is an independent, experimental implementation of XEN v1.7-inspired participation mechanics on IOTA Move. It is built for transparent execution and inspectable economics.

Is this the official XEN Network app?

No. This is not the official XEN app and is not affiliated with Xenworld LLC or Fair Crypto Foundation.

What does "claim rank" actually do?

Starting a mint captures your cRank and maturity schedule on-chain. Your eventual mint outcome depends on rank delta, selected term, protocol multipliers, and claim timing.

What determines mint outcomes?

Core levers are rank delta (global rank minus cRank), term length, AMP/EAA dynamics, and late-claim penalty if claimed after maturity.

How does max mint term work?

Max term is protocol-controlled: 100 days until global rank passes 5,000, then it grows with a logarithmic rule.

What is the late-claim penalty schedule?

Penalty bands: day 0=0%, day 1=1%, day 2=3%, day 3=8%, day 4=17%, day 5=35%, day 6=72%, day 7+=99%.

Is APY only for staking?

Yes. APY applies to staking math. Mint outcomes come from mint-specific variables and timing, not staking APY.

What is XEN here?

XEN is the token unit used by this IOTA Move implementation. It is not official Ethereum XEN.

What is the fee simulator?

A planning view for the term-based claim-rank fee that is enforced on-chain. It previews the exact fee before you sign.

Is this audited?

No. Current build is unaudited and experimental. Digital assets are risky and may lose value.
