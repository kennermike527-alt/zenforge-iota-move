Three questions this project must answer

If these three answers are weak, the design is weak — no matter how good the charts look.

1) What it’s trying to achieve

Build a fair participation token model where issuance comes from open rules and user timing, not privileged pre-allocation or insider mint paths.

2) What participants actually do

Claim rank, choose a wait term, then claim inside the reward window. You are explicitly trading time discipline for reward outcome.

3) How sustainability is tested

Run deterministic baseline + shock simulations (rank surges, late-claim waves, churn pressure) and track drift, penalties, and concentration to catch fragile parameters before scale.

Participant flow (litepaper-aligned)

Step 1 — Claim rank

Enter and lock a rank context that influences your reward difficulty.

Step 2 — Select term

Choose how long to wait. Longer terms generally shift rewards upward versus short-term claims.

Step 3 — Claim within window

Late claims are progressively penalized (pattern reaches 99% after 7 days late).

Sustainability (mechanics-first)

This is rule-driven pressure, not a promise chart.

What changes over time

AMP declines daily, APY declines over protocol age, and EAA bonus declines as rank increases.

What gets harder later

Later participants generally face lower rank bonus and less favorable timing conditions than early disciplined claimers.

How claim penalties enforce discipline

Late claim penalties ramp aggressively and can reduce net rewards to near-zero territory.

XEN FAQ & Primer

⚠️ This project is unaudited and experimental. Use at your own risk.

Is this project audited?

No. This project is currently unaudited and experimental. Treat all smart-contract and execution flows as high risk.

Is this a 1:1 copy of XEN Ethereum?

No. XENFORGE follows core litepaper principles (participation-driven rewards, term-based claiming, progressive penalties) but is adapted to an IOTA implementation and tooling stack.

What reward logic is this based on?

Litepaper-style reward mechanics combine rank delta, selected term, and time-dependent factors (AMP/EAA). This site explains the behavior and simulates outcomes; exact on-chain implementation can differ by deployment version.

Can I see my rank and early-rank bonus?

Yes. In the Degressive Fee Simulator, set current global rank and your claim rank. The page shows your rank delta, EAA-style early-rank bonus, and relative score versus a later (+100k rank) cohort.

Why is claiming time-critical?

In the litepaper model, late claim penalties rise progressively and hit 99% after 7 days late. The economic intent is to prevent invisible-claim ladders from being dumped at once.

What are “Scenario Outcomes” and “Difference vs default”?

Scenario outcomes are controlled what-if simulations. Difference vs default measures how far a stress case drifts from baseline behavior. Large persistent drift is a warning that parameters may need retuning.

Where do mint fees go?

Important: mint-fee routing shown here is currently a policy simulator only. ZenForge on-chain contracts in this build do not yet enforce mint-fee collection.

Does this include term limits and claim-window penalties?

Yes in principle: the model follows litepaper behavior where term choice, claim timing, and late-window penalties strongly affect net outcome. Implementation details may differ by contract revision.

Does this simulator include staking APY logic?

Not yet as a first-class chart. Current focus is claim dynamics, degressive fee policy, and stress resilience. Staking/APY views can be added next as a dedicated module.

Are these live on-chain metrics?

No. These are deterministic simulation artifacts used for design validation and strategy analysis.

How do I refresh data and connect wallet?

Refresh data with node xen-iota-move/scripts/build-web-data.mjs. Wallet connect/disconnect is in the Wallet Connection section.
